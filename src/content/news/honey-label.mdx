  <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-stone-800 dark:text-stone-200 mt-8">The Project</h3>

  <div class="prose prose-stone dark:prose-invert max-w-none">
    <p class="text-stone-700 dark:text-stone-300">
      Lost Creek Farm, a beautiful homestead in Pepin, Wisconsin, needed a honey label that captured the essence of their artisanal approach to beekeeping and their connection to the land. The farm was <Link href="https://web.archive.org/web/20230520021014/https://www.marthastewart.com/1541132/lost-creek-farm-pepin-wisconsin-weekend-home-tour" external>featured in Martha Stewart</Link>, showcasing their dedication to sustainable farming and traditional practices.
    </p>
  </div>

  <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-stone-800 dark:text-stone-200 mt-8">Design Inspiration</h3>

  <div class="prose prose-stone dark:prose-invert max-w-none">
    <p class="text-stone-700 dark:text-stone-300">
      The label draws inspiration from Scandinavian folk art, which felt appropriate given Wisconsin's strong Nordic heritage. The design features:
    </p>
    <ul class="text-stone-700 dark:text-stone-300">
      <li><strong>Decorative wildlife</strong> - Horses and deer representing the pastoral beauty of the farm</li>
      <li><strong>Botanical motifs</strong> - Hand-drawn flowers and foliage creating a border of natural elements</li>
      <li><strong>Bold typography</strong> - "LOST CREEK" prominently displayed with a mix of red and white lettering</li>
      <li><strong>Dark background</strong> - A deep navy/black base that makes the golden honey visible through the jar while providing strong contrast for the white design elements</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <h3 class="text-xl font-semibold text-stone-800 dark:text-stone-200 mt-8">Design Approach</h3>

  <div class="prose prose-stone dark:prose-invert max-w-none">
    <p class="text-stone-700 dark:text-stone-300">
      The folk art style serves multiple purposes: it differentiates the product on the shelf, communicates the artisanal nature of the honey, and tells a story about the farm's connection to traditional craftsmanship. The symmetrical composition and hand-drawn quality give it a timeless, authentic feel that resonates with consumers looking for locally-produced, high-quality honey.
    </p>
    <p class="text-stone-700 dark:text-stone-300">
      The location "Pepin, Wisconsin" is subtly integrated into the design, grounding the product in its geographic origin and appealing to those who value knowing where their food comes from.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>
